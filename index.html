Master's Thesis

Modeling the Temporal Persistence of Customer Sentiment from Online Reviews in E-Commerce Sales Forecasting

EDC Paris Business School · Master's in Data Science & Business Intelligence · 2026

This thesis is currently under review by my supervisor and pending final approval by EDC Paris Business School. The full document will be shared publicly once the process is complete. Thank you for your understanding.

Research Question

To what extent does the usefulness of customer sentiment from online reviews persist over time when incorporated into sales forecasting models in e-commerce contexts?

Data Model

Overview

This research adopts a quantitative and hybrid approach combining primary and secondary data to analyse how customer reviews influence purchasing decisions and sales forecasting in e-commerce. The study is structured around three main components: a consumer survey, a transactional analysis, and a machine learning modelling phase.

The first component is a primary survey with 174 respondents, designed to capture consumer perceptions on the credibility and temporal relevance of online reviews. The goal was to understand how people weigh reviews differently depending on how recent they are, and whether that perception translates into measurable purchasing behaviour.

The second component is a transactional analysis based on the Olist Brazilian e-commerce dataset, including orders, products and reviews. An ETL pipeline was built in Python to process over 100,000 transactions, consolidating and validating the data before any modelling could begin.

The third component is the modelling phase, which combines NLP and forecasting techniques. Sentiment classification was performed using BERTimbau fine-tuned on 2,700 manually labelled reviews. The forecasting model integrates an Attention-based BiLSTM with XGBoost to capture temporal patterns and correct residual errors. An ablation study was conducted across three model configurations to measure the incremental impact of review metrics and sentiment on predictive accuracy, evaluated through MAE, RMSE and WMAPE across forecasting horizons of 1, 7, 14 and 30 days.
